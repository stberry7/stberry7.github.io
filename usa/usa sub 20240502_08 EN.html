January 4, 2024 (Property: The 10 Wealthiest Cities in the United States)

The United States is home to many affluent communities tailored for the wealthy. These areas offer a safe environment, excellent schools, and a peaceful lifestyle. As of 2024, the 10 wealthiest cities in the US are as follows:

Atherton, California: Located near Silicon Valley, Atherton boasts a median household income of $534,000, which is seven times the national average. This small city of 6,950 residents has strict zoning regulations allowing only one house per acre, ensuring a comfortable and spacious living environment. It is primarily home to Silicon Valley tech executives, with an average home price of a staggering $7.25 million.

Cherry Hills Village, Colorado: Situated near Denver, Cherry Hills Village has a median income of $451,500 and an average home price of $2,975,000, making it one of the wealthiest and safest cities in Colorado. It offers excellent public schools, diverse recreational facilities, and upscale shopping malls.

Los Altos Hills, California: Located in the heart of Silicon Valley, Los Altos Hills has an average household income of $417,000. With beautiful natural scenery, a relaxed rural lifestyle, and convenient access to nearby cities, it is a popular choice among Silicon Valley's affluent.

Hillsborough, California: Near San Francisco, Hillsborough has an average household income of $373,100 and an average home price of $5.4 million. It offers many scenic spots for luxury shopping and dining amidst beautiful nature.

Short Hills, New Jersey: With a population of 15,000, Short Hills boasts a median income of $362,910 and an average home price of $1,910,792. Located within an hour's drive or train ride from the New York metropolitan area, it is a preferred upscale residential area for New York commuters. It offers excellent schools, safe neighborhoods, luxury shopping malls, and golf courses.

Highland Park, Texas: This affluent residential city near Dallas has an average household income of $354,905, which is five times the national average. In 2022, the average home price was a whopping $2,390,340. While situated near downtown Dallas, it offers a tranquil and spacious suburban lifestyle, making it an ideal choice for wealthy families.

Scarsdale, New York: Located in Westchester County, north of New York City, Scarsdale is an upscale residential city with an average household income of $352,350. Conveniently situated for commuting to New York, it attracts affluent working families with its beautiful natural environment and high-end restaurants.

Darien, Connecticut: One of the wealthiest cities in Connecticut, Darien has an average household income of $350,900 and an average home price of $1,642,800. This safe and pleasant coastal city is just an hour's train ride from New York, offering excellent public schools, beautiful natural surroundings, and top-notch restaurants and shopping facilities.

Winnetka, Illinois: Situated along the northern Chicago coast, Winnetka is an upscale residential area with a median income of $350,370 and an average home price of $1,298,750. While offering convenient commuting to Chicago, it provides a relaxed and safe suburban lifestyle, making it a beloved city among the affluent. In 2020, it was ranked the second-best place to live in the US.

Glencoe, Illinois: Located north of Chicago, Glencoe has an average income of $329,885, well above the national average. With its beautiful coastal scenery, parks, and lakes, as well as exceptional safety, this affluent family-friendly city offers a luxurious residential environment and convenient commuting to Chicago.

The common threads among these cities are median incomes and home prices that far exceed national averages, coupled with pleasant and safe living environments, quality education, convenient transportation, and ample leisure and entertainment facilities. Many are located near economic hubs like Silicon Valley or New York, offering scenic natural surroundings and a relaxed lifestyle.

The median incomes ranged from $329,000 (Glencoe) to $534,000 (Atherton), and the average home prices varied widely from $1.15 million (Glencoe) to $7.25 million (Atherton), but all were significantly higher than the national average. California had the most cities (4) on the list, with Atherton, Los Altos Hills, and Hillsborough. Three cities (Short Hills, Darien, and Scarsdale) were located near New York, in New Jersey, Connecticut, and New York State.

This reflects the fact that Silicon Valley and the New York metropolitan area are the greatest generators of wealth in the US, and the affluent seek to escape the bustling city centers in favor of nearby comfortable environments where they can enjoy a luxurious and dignified lifestyle.
